Aphek Woodruff
J. H. 1847, P. 42.

Entered Valley 25 September 1847 in A. O. Smoot Company.

He was born 11 November 1778 in Farmington, Connecticut, son of Eldad Woodruff and Diana Woodruff. Wilford Woodruff, son of Aphek Woodruff, says of his father, "My father married a second wife, Azubah Hart. She bore him six children. He was a man of strong constitution and did a great amount of labor. At eighteen years of age he began work in a flour mill and saw mill and continued at his occupation there for about fifty years. Most of that time he labored eighteen hours a day. He never made any profession of religion until I baptized him into the Church of Jesus Christ on the first day of July, 1838. He was a man of great charity, honesty, and integrity and made himself poor by giving to the poor. He was liberal in accommodating his fellow men by lending money and by becoming surety for his neighbors. He generally said yes to every man who asked a favor at his hands." Aphek Woodruff died 20 May 1861 in Salt Lake City.
