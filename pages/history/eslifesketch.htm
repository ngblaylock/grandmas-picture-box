Life Sketch of Eliza Spencer Moses
Utah Pioneer of 1861
By Josephine Pettit Groesbeck

Eliza Spencer was born June 15, 1813, in Ohio in the town. She was the first of Solomon and Martha Jones Spencer's children born in Ohio, and one of a family of eight children.

When Eliza was twelve years old her father died leaving his family to provide for themselves. It was a new country and the people suffered from malaria. She walked three miles to school and lived, meanwhile working for her board, with a family who provided her a home.

She had two sisters older than herself and one younger sister who married a Sectarian minister; She joined the Mormons, after which he didn't treat her very kindly. At the same time Eliza and her mother joined the church. Eliza had three brothers who never did join the church and were very much opposed to their mother and sister having done so.

Eliza lived with the family of a convert named Jonathan Taylor, who kept a hotel. While here she met Patriarch Smith, father of the Prophet, who gave per a blessing. She went with this family to Missouri but were not permitted to stay, and were driven to Nauvoo, Ill. Here she went into the family of Louis Robbins to help his wife-and while here a friend of the Robbins family, James Moses, came and met Eliza Spencer.

James Moses was a widower with two small children which were cared for by the Robbins family. A romance followed this meeting, and they were married at the first Conference in Nauvoo, by Don Carlos Smith. They lived in Nauvoo, and, here, four children were born to them. Three died, two boys and one girl. After the expulsion from Nauvoo they lived for a number of years in Iowa where three more children were born.

In 1853 the family moved to Canaen Conn. where one more child, Fred, was born-making five living children. They lived on the old Moses homestead, taking care of the old folks. After their death James Moses decided to move West, and in the summer of 1861 the family started West.

After a rather uneventful journey with its hardships, such as having the children sick with the measles, and her husband laid up with an infected foot, they arrived in Salt Lake Valley September 16, 1861.

When she married James Moses and joined the Mormons she cut herself off from her own family, who were Mormon opposers, but she bravely went forward and never had the pleasure of meeting any of them again, with the exception of one brother who was passing through Salt Lake City on the way to Oregon . This was the last contact she ever had with any of her kin, and it is not known by her children where any of them reside.

She lived with her family in Cottonwood near Cottonwood Bridge. She was actively engaged in Relief Society and other neighborhood activities.

The last two or three years of her life she was in poor health and on December 15, 1874 she passed from this life, mourned by her family and a large circle of friends and neighbors.

The following winter after leaving Nauvoo they lived in Winter Quarters Neb.

She raised to manhood and womanhood five children:

Martha E., born in Nauvoo

Jesse Tilton, born at Council Bluffs Iowa , and married Arabell Woodruff

Eliza Ann, born at Council Bluffs , and married to Joseph Newman.

James Spencer, born in Council Bluffs , and died in 1886

Frederick Filmore, born at Canaen Conn.

All these were pioneers of 1861.
