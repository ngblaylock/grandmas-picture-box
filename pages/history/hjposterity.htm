Hans and Dorthea Jorgensen Posterity
By Ruth Ballard Dance, Mary’s granddaughter

We should be proud of their blood running through our veins, For a stalwart heritage who made possible, us grasping life’s reins. Eight children blessed this couple with standards high, A family that has helped bring happiness to many, we can’t deny. Names from the Bible were chosen to be one of the children’s crowns, And they proudly did their names justice, whether they wore pants or gowns

Don’t we feel proud of JACOB who proved to be a pioneer in every way? And of his helpmate Melvina, ever by his side day by day, Encouraging him and both being brave to help break traditions that "this isn’t possible", you’ll lose your shirt Jake. For it took men and women like this to prove "it can be done", People who weren’t afraid of hard work, sweat and the feel of discouragement's gun. How the posterity of these two Jorgensen’s must fill with pride, When they hear stories how Jake and Melvina took life in their stride.

ISAAC, must have made his parents smile as his talent unfolded to them, A worker in the rich soil that will ever produce a new gem. One of God’s faithful women was also a great gift to this man, Together they worked and schemed to help develop God’s great plan. Their children were so welcome and truth prevailed at every turn, They worked as a unit in those long ago days and did yearn To give their posterity a candle to work by all, their lives through, Whether they be doctors, lawyers, farmers or whatever they chose to do. For they realized in life its worth, and the everlasting beauty, And as Mom and Dad or Aunt Rebecca and Uncle Isaac they did their duty. So we salute this family head who stands in line for a vote of thanks, And who, wherever they are in righteousness, hold high ranks.

ABRAHAM also did his name proud, although not in worldly fame, But in worthwhile, useful achievement just the same. Aunt Minnie thought him an awfully good person to know,

As their relationship grew into years you could feel this same glow. A glow that their children, and children’s children will ever speak of with respect,

For they are so proud of their pioneer heritage who ever stood erect, On every principle that helps to make family bonds tied with a knot, Ties, that even with all the money and fame possible, cannot be bought. How thankful they must feel to be born of such goodly stock, To know their "houses were not built of sand but of solid rock."

JOSEPH came along and wasn’t missing any of his brothers traits, He also did his part in letting God guide him through good gates. The Jorgensen’s name carried down through Uncle Joe and Aunt Janies family tree Can boast of farmers and business men who the world could desire to I’ll bet this posterity have many wonderful tales to tell, Of a big yellow brick house where children in fun did yell. Also, how they were trained to appreciate mother nature’s pleasures As they plowed, harrowed, irrigated and hoed their parents treasures So the memory of Joe and Jennie to this fine family is revered, They must feel humble thanks today, and often, for how they were reared.

MOSES was a name Hans and Dorthea also did choose, But who would ever deny Uncle Moses not filling the shoes. He and Aunt Rue had their heartaches and joys with the rest, But could somehow interpret it as being for the best. Nanna knew a home with them that must fill her with love, Recognizing they were chosen spirits coming from a high above. Patient, kind and with a sense of humor too. Couldn’t we all gain from their lives an important life's clue The feminine side of the family we will now interview.

MARIA, a strong personality of which today there are few. Never too tired or busy to be an angel of mercy to the sick, There wasn’t a problem or disaster that with her outlook on life; couldn’t lick She homesteaded for two husbands and children she held dear, Teaching them if they lived righteously they had nothing to fear. there was always roan at her house for one more to come and abide, Many have profited by wise advice given while working or visiting by her side. Her posterity take their places proudly in the world today, Excelling in their chosen fields, whether farmers, or if they have a new road to lay. She knew the meaning of physical labor and even pain before she passed away, But to a reward saved just for her; for accomplishments in life lived day by day.

MARY. She chose a God-fearing man to walk by her side ever steady, Anything put before then to work out together they were always ready. Whether it was for their children, who came in an even dozen, Or for the happiness of anyone or anything) be it an animal or cousin. their doors always bad Welcome written across the wood, People were meant to be happy here, at least they did all they could. Family projects, family dinners and family jokes were ever a circle round, In that Big Red house there was seldom a quiet sound. Aunt Mary could cook food to bring out the flavor divine, As well as cleverly teach truth and kindness to the utmost sublime. To be able to call her that precious word Grandma, friend, mother or aunt, to describe what that choice spirit stands for to her posterity, I can’t. How proud these two Monks would be to view the abilities of posterity they love so, Doctors, dairy men, business men, scholars, bishops, and so many who their church love and know. Would bring tears of thankfulness to their eyes as at each they would smile Immediately planning a farni1y dinner; after all they should be busy and doing something worthwhile.

REBECCA describes a very patient, kind and unselfish person when brought to our family test, She so helped to influence the privileged people who knew her the very best. Her talents were not hidden but developed and grew no end, Especially when those things she knew were right she stood to defend. She proved to be such a wise choice for Uncle Pete Frost to desire, And was the kind of mother and neighbor anyone would like to acquire. She taught good things every day of her life through, To her own flesh and blood, or peoples of every hue.

She had a desire to help her fellow man in any fix, Using her many gifts and talents that some people called "some tricks." The Hans and Dorthea family wouldn’t have been complete without this accomplished lass, To her wonderful, devout family, the name, Rebecca in this whole Jorgensen family heads the class.

Oh! shouldn’t we feel grateful for relatives so strong and true, They hold a challenging example before me and you. God Bless them for the indelible memories they’ve left behind, Where could we have chosen a better heritage of any kind?

By Ruth Ballard Dance, Mary’s granddaughter
