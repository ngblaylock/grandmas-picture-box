Missionary Blessing of Lola Everett Jorgensen
Given upon the head of Elder Lora Everett Jorgensen, in the Salt Lake Temple Annex, September 12th, 1905, by President Christian D. Fieldsted.

Lola E. Jorgensen, we place our hands upon your head and set you apart for a mission to Scandinavia, whereunto you have been called by the prophet of god, and we seal upon you the spirit of this calling and say unto you, inasmuch as your heart's desire is to serve the: Lord and keep his commandments and live pure and holy, the spirit of this office and calling shall rest upon you and you shall be able to preach the gospel of life and salvation to those whom you will come in contact. The Lord will bless you and give you the language to convey your ideas and he will raise up friends unto you who will help you and feed you and give you a place of shelter and receive you in their houses. You shall be blessed and strengthened by day and by night, and the Lord will reveal unto you things that will comfort you, and you shall feel that the Lord is with you and help you and strengthen you, and you shall be able to overcome the weaknesses of your nature, and you shall have strength in your body and marrow in your bones. You shall be able to perform this mission to your own satisfaction, for the Lord will help you in every respect if you will keep yourself near unto Him. Subject yourself to the authorities of the mission and be kind to your traveling companions, and you shall always be on the safe side of the question, and when the Lord blesses you with talent, always give the honor and glory to Him. Go forth and spare not; lift up your voice and proclaim the gospel and warn the inhabitants of the nation where you shall labor, that they may understand that the kingdom of the Son of God is near at hand. Testify that Joseph Smith was a prophet of the living God in these last days and that this is the gospel of life and salvation that you are bearing to them. We say unto you, dear brother, inasmuch as you will keep yourself pure, the Lord will bless you and give you many friends and you shall rejoice and feel that these are the best days of your life. We say unto you, dear brother, that you shall go in peace and return in safety and be blessed in every respect.

We seal upon you every blessing that your heart can desire in righteousness and reseal upon you all your former blessings and ordinations, and we say unto you, receive this call and go forth and be a servant of the living God in truth and deed. These blessings, with all necessary blessings to qualify you for this mission, we seal upon you in the name of Jesus, even so, amen.

Martin S. Lindsay,

Reporter
